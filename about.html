Biography

I am currently an undergraduate student at UCLA, studying Mathematics/Economics. I intend to graduate in June 2022, and will work as a research assistant at the Federal Reserve Board for two years, before applying to PhD programs in economics.

I have extensive research experience, beginning from my first year of college. I have worked as a research assistant for then-PhD student Zhihao Xu, Melanie Wasserman (UCLA Anderson), Michela Giorcelli (UCLA), and Martha Bailey (UCLA). In the summer of 2021, I interned at the Federal Reserve Board, where I worked in the Division of International Finance, under the supervision of Dario Caldara and Anna Lipinska.

I have researched in applied microeconomics, economic history, and international economics. My current projects study Head Start, an early education program for disadvantaged youth in the U.S., and currency crises in developing countries.

In my free time, I love to cook and play board games.
